What skills should an embedded systems engineer actually have? The field is broad enough that even experienced practitioners have significant blind spots. This article surveys several published roadmaps — including the ACM's 2016 Curriculum Guidelines for Computer Engineering and a curated set of book and course recommendations — as a way to take stock of where you are and identify what you don't know you don't know.

The ACM's recommendations cover the expected core topics: microcontroller architecture, peripherals (GPIO, UART, SPI, I2C), multitasking basics, and networking. The article builds a broader skill inventory around those foundations, touching on systems programming, real-time systems, analog electronics, and the softer skills that often separate good engineers from great ones. Recommended books for each area range from Elecia White's *Making Embedded Systems* to Michael Barr's *Programming Embedded Systems* and others, each vetted by the author's own experience teaching and consulting.

Whether you are a student who just finished a first Arduino project or a professional with decades of experience, periodically measuring yourself against a defined baseline is one of the most reliable ways to keep growing. The roadmaps here are deliberately opinion-driven — and I say so up front — which makes them more useful as a conversation starter than as a rigid prescription.
