I got to be on the Embedded.fm podcast! Show notes below.

>Nathan Jones chatted with us about his proposal for a computer architecture book based on a 4-bit computer.<br><br>Nathan found the 4-bit computer in the [Hackaday SuperCon 2022 badge](https://hackaday.com/2022/10/12/the-2022-supercon-badge-is-a-handheld-trip-through-computing-history/) and was amazed by some of the ideas that folks implemented (see [SuperCon Badge Hacking Awards Ceremony](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X-XJmlMLx7k)).<br><br>Nathan [spoke at Hackaday SuperCon 2023 on the processor architecture](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUcKpRigZts), highlighting some of his ideas for a book. If you’d like to try your hand at the badge, find it on Nathan’s [Voja4 Tindie page](https://www.tindie.com/products/0xc0decafe/supercon-2022-badge-voja4/).<br><br>Nathan also spoke recently at the Embedded Online Conference ([Building a Simple CLI](https://embeddedonlineconference.com/session/Building_a_Simple_Command_Line_Interface), [OOP in C](https://embeddedonlineconference.com/session/Object_Oriented_Programming_in_C), and [The Power of a Look-up Table](https://embeddedonlineconference.com/session/The_Power_of_a_Look_up_Table)) and the Teardown Conference ([Making Your Own MCU Boards}(https://www.crowdsupply.com/teardown/portland-2023/short-talk/make-your-own-mcu-boards) and [Building a Simple CLI](https://www.crowdsupply.com/teardown/portland-2024/short-talk/building-a-simple-command-line-interface)).<br><br>If you have an idea you’d like to propose, check out O’Reilly’s [proposal for a book or class](https://www.oreilly.com/work-with-us.html). While you may not go with them, the proposal is a good place to get all of your ideas down.<br><br>We mentioned a few other computer architecture books as competitors for Nathan’s proposed book:<br><br>• [Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach](https://www.amazon.com/Computer-Architecture-Quantitative-John-Hennessy/dp/012383872X) by John L. Hennessy and David A. Patterson<br>• [Introduction to Computing Systems: From bits & gates to C & beyond](https://www.amazon.com/Introduction-Computing-Systems-Gates-Beyond/dp/0072467509) by Yale N. Patt and Sanjay J. Patel<br>• [The Elements of Computing Systems](https://www.amazon.com/Elements-Computing-Systems-second-Principles/dp/0262539802), by Noam Nisan and Shimon Schocken (MIT Press) with supporting material and simulator on [nand2tetris](https://www.nand2tetris.org/).<br><br>Nathan also did a survey of the Embedded Slack community.
